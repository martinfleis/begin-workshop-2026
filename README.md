## The role of space in predictive models

Predictive modelling is becoming a ubiquitous tool in social sciences. Yet, most of the
models are not spatially aware in their nature, rendering their use on geographic data
suboptimal without further steps. This workshop illustrates which steps could be made to
treat spatial dimension as the first-class citizen in commonly used predictive models.
In the first part, we will learn how to use space to evaluate the behaviour of models.
Are they reliable? Do they showcase spatially unstable performance? How do we avoid
leakage of information through space? In the second part, we will learn how to embed
space in the models themselves. How to make use of spatial dependence? Can we capture
spatial heterogeneity? Can we use geographical context to improve the model? All these
questions will be answered using an interactive Python session. However, the concepts
are general and transferable. Basic prior knowledge of Python and geography is expected,
but the coding part is not the crucial component of the workshop. Just a vehicle to
learn about space, modelling, and how to deal with it.
